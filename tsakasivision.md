# Tsa Kasi Deliveries: Vision & Constitution

## WHO WE ARE

**Positioning Statement:**
Tsa Kasi makes the township economy visible. Customers order from stores that exist near them. Stores fulfill predictably. Agents execute delivery as skilled work. No algorithms pretending to be magic. No convenience theater. Infrastructure instead of hype.

---

## THE WORK WE DO

We bridge three broken connections:

**Customers** think nearby stores don't exist online. They spend time and transport money hunting, or they stop shopping.

**Stores** think they can't scale past their door without capital-heavy delivery operations. They are invisible to customers who have phones and money.

**Agents** are told delivery is chaos—underpaid gig labor with no structure. They accept invisible work because visible alternatives don't exist.

We make each real to the others.

## MISSION & OPERATIONS

### The Problem We Solve

**For township customers:**
You have money. You have a smartphone with data. You have a bank account. But you cannot order from nearby stores online, and you cannot receive predictable delivery. You spend time and transport money hunting. You become invisible to formal commerce.

**For township stores:**
You have customers. You have inventory. But you lack digital ordering infrastructure and delivery logistics. You cannot compete with what exists in suburbs. You remain stuck at your door.

**For delivery agents:**
You have mobility and local area knowledge. But the work available is invisible, unstable, and underpaid. You are told to be grateful for gig labor without structure.

### How We Solve It

**Centralized ordering platform** — One place to order from multiple stores nearby. No app per store.

**Direct online payments to stores** — Payments are traceable. No float. No cash hiding. Stores know their revenue immediately.

**Stores prepare orders** — We don't shop. Stores fulfill. This makes the system scale without us hiring packers.

**Agents pick up and deliver** — Clear scope. Clear pay. Clear completion state. This makes it skilled work, not invisible labor.

**Distance-based delivery pricing** — Pricing reflects reality. Closer orders are cheaper. Customers understand why.

### The Stakeholder Model

| **Stakeholder** | **Contributes** | **Receives** | **Success Metric** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Customers** | Demand, Payment | Convenience, Privacy, Time savings | Repeat usage |
| **Stores** | Inventory, Order Preparation, Fulfillment Reliability | Incremental revenue, No marketing cost, No delivery burden | Order volume, Retention (not churn) |
| **Agents** | Mobility, Local Knowledge, Service Execution | Predictable work, Clear pay, Transparent scope | Completion rate, Availability, Hourly consistency |
| **Platform** | Ordering system, Payment processing, Store coordination | Commission per order | Gross order value, Net margin, Store retention rate |

## BUSINESS MODEL & UNIT ECONOMICS

### Revenue Structure

**Customer pays:** Store price + Distance-based delivery fee

**Platform takes:** Commission per completed order (store-paid or blended)

**Agent receives:** Per-delivery payout (distance-based) + completion bonus for reliability

### Example: Standard Delivery Order

- Store goods: R200
- Delivery fee (4km): R25
- Customer total: R225
- Platform commission: 12% × R200 = R24
- Agent payout: R18 (base) + R2 (completion bonus) = R20
- Store net: R200 - R24 = R176

### Scaling Economics

**Fixed costs:**
- Platform hosting
- Core team (operations, support, audit)
- Store onboarding

**Variable costs:**
- Agent payouts (scale with orders)
- Payment processing fees
- Support load (scale with customer volume)

**How we scale without linear cost growth:**

1. **More orders per route** — Same agent, multiple deliveries, same fixed cost spread across more orders
2. **Store density** — More stores in same geography = more orders per km, higher agent utilization
3. **Platform reuse** — Same infrastructure per township; only add staff for support and store ops

### Growth Milestones

1. **MVP stability** — 50 orders/day, 85%+ on-time, 8%+ store repeat
2. **First profitable route** — One neighborhood reaches +15% net margin; agent completes 12+ orders/day
3. **First profitable township** — Four routes break even; reinvestment possible without external capital
4. **Multi-township expansion** — Playbook proven; replicate in adjacent townships

### Growth Without External Capital

1. Commission reinvests into support and operations
2. Tight operational control prevents bloat
3. Gradual geographic expansion (one route at a time)
4. Stores and agents compound loyalty month over month

## CONSTITUTION: INVIOLABLE RULES

These cannot be compromised. Not for growth. Not for funding. Not for convenience.

### Article I — Payment Integrity

Every order must be traceable to a phone number, a store, an agent, and a completed delivery. Manual cash is invisible cash. We do not operate invisible. 

**Implication:** We process payments only through traceable channels. No offline cash. No float. No hidden revenue.

---

### Article II — The Agent Question

Agents are not underpaid gig workers performing invisible labor.

Every delivery must have a clear payee, a clear completion state, and a completion record. If an agent completes 15 deliveries in a day, they have 15 completion records—not a vague "worked today" fee.

If an agent's work cannot be measured, the system is broken.

**Implication:** We measure completion rates, timeliness, and customer feedback per agent. We pay based on measured completion, not hours worked. Agents are visible, paid for visible work.

---

### Article III — Store Trust

Stores retain customers only if orders complete reliably.

If we cannot guarantee that Tsa Kasi is easier and more reliable than doing nothing, we do not scale. Growth through compromised reliability is death.

**Implication:** We measure on-time delivery, order accuracy, and repeat usage per store. If a store's orders are dropping into chaos, we fix it immediately or delist them.

---

### Article IV — Geographic Realism

We operate in geographies we understand. We do not expand into territories where we cannot operate.

Expanding into a township requires: local agent base ready, partner stores confirmed, delivery infrastructure feasible, and support team capacity available.

**Implication:** We expand one route at a time. We do not blanket-cover an area and hope delivery works.

---

### Article V — No Shortcuts on Pricing

Distance-based pricing is transparent. Customers see what they pay for.

We do not use surge pricing to hide margin. We do not discount delivery to artifially drive volume. We do not take promotional losses that signal unsustainability.

**Implication:** Pricing reflects actual cost. When margins tighten, we fix operations, not pricing.

---

### Article VI — Data Ownership & Privacy

Customer data belongs to customers. We do not monetize it. We do not sell it.

Tsa Kasi knows what you ordered, where you live, and when you shop. This is a private fact, not a business asset.

**Implication:** We encrypt customer data. We retain minimally. We support user deletion on request. We never sell insights to third parties.

---

### Article VII — Support Is Real

When an order fails, a real support person unbreaks it.

We do not respond with chatbots, FAQs, or deflections. If a customer paid for a delivery that did not arrive, support investigates and resolves.

**Implication:** Support is staffed. It is measured by resolution time, not response speed. It is held accountable for customer outcomes.

---

### Article VIII — No Misaligned Partnerships

We refuse partnerships that compromise mission.

If a partner asks us to cut safety corners, misrepresent delivery times, or monetize customer data, we say no and walk away. Growth through misalignment is not growth; it's business decay.

**Implication:** We say no more than we say yes. Every partnership is reviewed against These Articles.

---

### Article IX — Audit and Adherence

We audit order completion, delivery accuracy, agent timeliness, store satisfaction, and payment integrity monthly.

If we cannot measure it, we cannot defend it. If we cannot defend it, it is not policy; it is failure.

**Implication:** Every metric is documented. Every anomaly is investigated. Every decision is traceable.

---

### Article X — Principle Over Growth

When principle and growth conflict, principle wins.

We do not compromise reliability for scale. We do not compromise agent fairness for margin. We do not compromise customer privacy for data revenue.

**Implication:** We grow at the pace we can sustain with integrity. We say no to deals that require Articles I–IX to break.
