# Response Integrity: Canonical Holistic Operating Model

This is the canonical description of Response Integrity as a brand, a business, an operation, and a rhythm.

It keeps the public-facing positioning separate from the internal operating reality, while still describing how both parts work together.

## 1. Brand

Response Integrity is publicly positioned as a calm-performance academic support brand.

The public story is simple: we help learners build structured execution before pressure arrives.
The brand should feel steady, reassuring, and serious, not noisy, rushed, or generic.
It should feel like calm-performance culture: preparation before panic, structure before stress, and evidence before confidence.

### Public-facing positioning

The clearest public lines are:

- We Train Calm Execution. Under Pressure.
- Math is the training arena.
- Calm First. Confidence follows.
- School introduces. Response Integrity conditions.

Those lines should carry the outward brand voice. Families should understand that Response Integrity prepares learners before stress appears, instead of trying to patch panic after it has already started.

### Brand essence

The public story is that Response Integrity helps learners:

- think more clearly
- stay structured when difficulty appears
- build confidence through evidence
- stay calm when the work gets hard
- develop repeatable execution instead of one-off performance
- move through pressure with less panic and more control

That is the brand promise at its cleanest level.

### What families should feel

When a parent reads or hears about Response Integrity, they should feel:

- this is serious, but not scary
- this is structured, but not cold
- this is disciplined, but still supportive
- my child is being prepared, not judged
- this is about real change, not temporary encouragement
- this is calm-performance support, not panic response

### Public-facing promise

The public-facing promise is not just that the learner will receive sessions. It is that the learner will receive consistent attention, structured repetition, and good pressure in a safe environment.

- individual attention at the learner's pace
- a safe space to make mistakes
- good pressure through observation, not shame
- execution under difficulty
- preparation before pressure arrives
- a system-led method rather than personality-led tutoring

### Public-facing themes

The public-facing language should keep returning to a few stable themes:

- the learner gets direct attention
- the learner is seen clearly
- mistakes are part of training
- structure matters more than hype
- pressure is prepared for early
- calm execution is the goal
- confidence comes from evidence
- small structured pods protect focus and consistency

### Public-facing tone

The public voice should sound:

- human, clear, and grounded
- reassuring without being vague
- disciplined without being harsh
- confident without hype
- parent-safe and easy to understand
- direct without sounding cold

### Current public audience

The visible family-facing brand is currently centered on Grades 6-9.

That does not mean the operating logic cannot expand later, but the public story stays simple and parent-safe.

### What the brand is not

Response Integrity is not framed as:

- casual homework help
- reactive rescue tutoring
- a loose chat-based study service
- a personality-led teaching business
- last-minute panic support
- a noisy cram service
- a vague confidence-booster
- a one-size-fits-all lesson factory

Instead, the public story is that Response Integrity trains calm execution under pressure.

### The public narrative in one sentence

Response Integrity is a calm-performance system for Grades 6-9 learners, built to train structured execution before pressure arrives.

## 2. Business model

The current live family offer is a monthly subscription.

### Live pricing language

- Monthly subscription per student: `R1000`
- Standard paid family offer: monthly `8-session` package
- Default cadence: `2 sessions per week`

The parent-facing UI uses **Premium plan** language.
The legal and billing docs describe the same live offer as the standard paid family package.

The older “standard / premium / pro” ladder should not be treated as the current live truth.
The code currently surfaces one paid family offer plus pilot-access exceptions, not a broad consumer tier stack.

### Payment logic

- payment is tied to the monthly package
- sessions are allocated in the fixed monthly rhythm
- carryover does not happen by default unless explicitly granted
- some enrollments can enter through pilot or invitation-based access before standard payment flow begins

### Revenue split

- Tutor: `R750`
- Platform: `R250`

So the business is not only a tutoring service; it is a recurring subscription model with an operational split per learner.

### Pod economics

The current operating pod is:

- `12 tutors`
- `36 students`
- `3 students per tutor`
- `288 sessions per month`

Per pod:

- total monthly revenue collected: `36 x R1000 = R36,000`
- tutor payouts: `36 x R750 = R27,000`
- platform revenue: `36 x R250 = R9,000`
- workspace cost: `R864`
- net platform income before marketing and admin: `R8,136`

This pod is the repeatable operational and financial unit.

### Current pod size vs future scale

The scale model in the vision docs may grow later, but the current live pod remains:

- 12 tutors
- 36 students

That is the number to treat as current operational truth.

#### Commercial onboarding

1. parent creates an account and completes the form
2. fit is reviewed

5. diagnosis and proposal logic run

## 6. Tutor operating model

Tutors are system executors.

They do not run the system from instinct.
They run it from the current topic state.

### Tutor lifecycle

The tutor pathway is:

1. applicant
2. training
3. sandbox
4. certified live

### Tutor mode states

The tutor can be in:

- applicant mode
- training mode
- sandbox mode
- certified live mode

### What the tutor workspace contains

The tutor’s live operating surface is the topic-conditioning map.

The map shows:

- Topic
- Current Phase
- Current Stability
- Status
- Next Move
- Constraint
- Tutor meaning
- Parent meaning
- System direction

### What the tutor actually does

The tutor:

- opens the correct topic surface
- runs the correct drill for the current context
- records what the student actually did
- avoids rescue unless the protocol allows it
- protects the phase rules
- updates the system honestly
- keeps the parent and student surfaces in sync with the real state

### Training mode vs live mode

When the tutor is in training mode:

- live scheduling is disabled
- the tutor uses the training surfaces instead of booked Google Meet lesson windows
- intro and training drills happen inside the student workflow surfaces

When the tutor is certified live:

- live scheduling is available
- the pod assignment opens the live operating layer for students, onboarding, conditioning, and reports

## 7. Tutor tools

Three tools are central to the tutor operating model.

### 7.1 3-Layer Lens

The 3-Layer Lens is always on.

It checks:

- vocabulary
- method
- reason

It is the main diagnosis and correction lens across the whole system.

### 7.2 Model -> Apply -> Guide

MAG is not universal.

It belongs mainly in Clarity and concept-entry work.

In that lane:

- model the concept
- let the student apply immediately
- guide the correction through the lens

### 7.3 Boss Battles

Boss Battles start in Controlled Discomfort.

They become timed in Time Pressure Stability.

They are introduced when the learner’s current phase and drill design call for them, not when the tutor feels like making the session harder.

### Tool map by phase

| Phase | Main tool pattern | What it is doing |
|---|---|---|
| Clarity | MAG + 3-Layer Lens | recognition, naming, first-step understanding |
| Structured Execution | 3-Layer Lens | independent starts, step order, repeatable method |
| Controlled Discomfort | 3-Layer Lens + Boss Battles | harder or unfamiliar work, no rescue, first-step only |
| Time Pressure Stability | 3-Layer Lens + timed Boss Battles | timed structure under urgency |

## 8. Topic conditioning

Topic Conditioning is the core academic engine.

The system turns a real school topic into the arena where Response Integrity trains the student’s response.

### What the system is asking

It does not only ask:

- does the student know this topic?

It asks:

- In this topic, where does the student break?
- How does the student behave when difficulty appears?
- Can the student stay clear?
- Can the student execute?
- Can the student hold when uncertainty appears?
- Can the student stay structured under time pressure?

### Topic structure

Each topic is tracked with:

- Topic
- Phase
- Stability

The phase track is:

`Clarity -> Structured Execution -> Controlled Discomfort -> Time Pressure Stability`

### Important rule

A student does not have one global phase.

They have a phase per topic.

That means one learner can be:

- stable in exponents
- unstable in algebra
- strong in fractions
- weak in word problems

That is not random inconsistency.
That is topic-specific response state.

## 9. The four phases

### 9.1 Clarity

In Clarity, the student learns to see the topic clearly.

The student should be able to:

- name the terms
- identify the problem type
- state the steps
- explain why the steps work

The system asks:

- Can the student clearly see what they are dealing with in this topic?

### 9.2 Structured Execution

In Structured Execution, the student must execute inside the topic.

The student should be able to:

- start without delay
- follow steps in order
- reduce guessing
- repeat the method reliably

The system asks:

- Can the student act reliably in this topic without being carried?

### 9.3 Controlled Discomfort

In Controlled Discomfort, difficulty is introduced inside the topic.

The student faces:

- harder questions
- unfamiliar forms
- no rescue
- first-step guidance only

The system asks:

- Can the student stay stable in this topic when certainty disappears?

### 9.4 Time Pressure Stability

In Time Pressure Stability, the same topic is tested under time.

The student works with:

- timed attempts
- process under pressure
- structure maintained under urgency

The system asks:

- Can the student stay structured in this topic when time pressure appears?

## 10. Session context and drill type

The session engine keeps the context and the drill separate.

### Session contexts

- Intro
- Active Training
- Tutor Handover Verification

### Drill types

- Diagnosis
- Training
- Verification

### What each context means

- **Intro**: placement. Find the starting phase in a real topic.
- **Active Training**: conditioning. Train the current topic using the correct drill for its state.
- **Tutor Handover Verification**: continuity. Confirm inherited state and continue cleanly.

### What the tutor must not do

- do not pick drills from instinct
- do not treat intro like ordinary teaching
- do not restart a student during handover
- do not record invented improvement
- do not let the drill become vague support

The system chooses the drill.
The tutor executes it.

## 11. Progress and breakdown

### What progress means

Progress is not just:

- more correct answers
- faster answers
- looking more confident

Progress means the student’s response inside the topic is changing.

### Signs of progress

- faster orientation
- earlier first step
- reduced guessing
- staying inside the problem longer
- cleaner language
- self-correction
- emotional stabilization
- better performance under pressure

### What breakdown means

Breakdowns fall into two broad types.

#### Cognitive breakdowns

- vocabulary
- method
- reason

#### Response breakdowns

- freeze
- rush
- guessing
- help-seeking dependence

That distinction matters because the tutor must know whether the issue is:

- the student does not understand the concept layer
- the student cannot yet regulate the response layer

## 14. Service delivery rhythm

This is what life looks like in the operating system over time.

### Parent / student life

1. family enters the intake window
2. the form identifies the real topics and real struggle patterns
3. intro diagnosis places each topic
4. the proposal translates the state into a training plan
5. parent accepts
6. sessions begin in the fixed weekly cadence
7. the student dashboard updates as work is logged
8. the parent receives weekly updates
9. the parent receives monthly reports
10. the topic state progresses only when the data says it should

### Tutor life

1. tutor enters through applicant mode
2. tutor is conditioned and checked
3. tutor becomes sandbox-ready
4. tutor becomes certified live
5. tutor receives pod assignment
6. tutor opens the topic map and student cards
7. tutor runs the correct drill in the correct phase
8. tutor logs the actual student response
9. tutor updates reports and movement markers
10. TD oversight checks compliance and drift

### Rhythm principle

The system is designed so the learner is never just “doing work.”

They are being conditioned through a paced response rhythm:

- recognition
- repetition
- discomfort
- timing
- transfer
