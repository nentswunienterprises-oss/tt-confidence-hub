TT-OS BATTLE TEST: STRUCTURED EXECUTION (SCORING VERSION)
🔹 SECTION A — CORE UNDERSTANDING
Q1

What is the purpose of Structured Execution in TT, in one sentence?

Expected Answer (signal):
To ensure the student can execute a known method independently, in order, and repeatedly without assistance.

Fail Answer:

“To help the student practice solving”
“To improve accuracy”
“To reinforce understanding”
Q2

What is the key difference between Clarity and Structured Execution?

Expected Answer:
Clarity verifies recognition (seeing the problem).
Structured Execution verifies independent action (doing the method correctly without help).

Fail Answer:

“Clarity is easier, execution is harder”
“Clarity is theory, execution is practice”
Q3

Why does TT enforce “state steps before solving”?

Expected Answer:
To force structured thinking and prevent guessing or skipping. It ensures the student is consciously following the method.

Fail Answer:

“To help them remember”
“To slow them down”
Q4

Why is guessing not tolerated in this phase?

Expected Answer:
Because guessing hides lack of structure. The system is training reliable execution, not lucky outcomes.

Fail Answer:

“Because it causes mistakes”
“Because it’s bad practice”
Q5

Why is tutor opinion irrelevant in progression out of Structured Execution?

Expected Answer:
Because progression is based on observed execution signals and repeatable performance, not how the tutor feels.

Fail Answer:

“To keep things consistent”
“To follow system rules” (too surface-level)
🔹 SECTION B — APPLICATION
Q6

A student knows the method but waits for help before starting.
👉 What phase are they in and why?

Expected Answer:
Structured Execution. They cannot initiate independently despite knowing the method.

Fail Answer:

Clarity
“They lack confidence”
Q7

During the cold start window, the student hesitates.
👉 What must you NOT do?

Expected Answer:
Do not help, guide, or prompt. The hesitation must be observed and logged.

Fail Answer:

“Encourage them to start”
“Give a small hint”
Q8

A student gets the correct answer but skips steps.
👉 Do you accept or reject the rep? Why?

Expected Answer:
Reject. Correct answer without structure is not valid execution.

Fail Answer:

“Accept, they got it right”
“Correct them next time”
Q9

A student executes correctly once but breaks on the next similar problem.
👉 What does this indicate?

Expected Answer:
Lack of repeatability. Execution is not yet stable.

Fail Answer:

“They made a small mistake”
“They just need more practice”
Q10

Why does TT include Variation Control in this phase?

Expected Answer:
To test whether the student can apply the same method when the form changes, preventing memorization.

Fail Answer:

“To challenge the student”
“To make it harder”
🔹 SECTION C — PRESSURE SCENARIOS
Scenario 1

Tutor sees the student struggling to start and gives a hint after 3 seconds.
👉 What violation occurred?

Expected Answer:
Breaking the cold start window. The student was not allowed to initiate independently.

Fail Answer:

“Helping the student”
“Keeping the flow going”
Scenario 2

Student asks: “Is this the right step?” before continuing.
👉 What do you do?

Expected Answer:
Refuse confirmation and require the student to proceed using their own structure.

Fail Answer:

“Confirm to keep them on track”
“Guide them slightly”
Scenario 3

Tutor keeps re-modelling the method during execution reps.
👉 What is the problem?

Expected Answer:
The tutor is reverting to teaching instead of enforcing independent execution.

Fail Answer:

“Helping reinforce learning”
Scenario 4

Student completes all reps correctly but relies on small tutor prompts throughout.
👉 Do you progress or hold? Why?

Expected Answer:
Hold. Independence is not achieved.

Fail Answer:

“Progress, they got them right”
🔹 SECTION D — RED FLAG RESPONSES

Auto-fail signals:

❌ “They got the answer right, so it’s fine”
→ Ignores structure

❌ “I would guide them a bit at the start”
→ Breaks cold start

❌ “They just need confidence to start alone”
→ Avoids execution problem

❌ “Steps aren’t always necessary if they understand”
→ Breaks system completely

❌ “I’d help them until they’re comfortable”
→ Tutor dependency mindset
