TT-OS BATTLE TEST: TOPIC CONDITIONING (SCORING VERSION)
🔹 SECTION A — CORE UNDERSTANDING
Q1

Explain Topic Conditioning without using the words:
“topic”, “phase”, or “stability”.

Expected Answer (signal):
Describes training how a student behaves when difficulty appears inside the work they’re currently doing, identifying where they break, and repeatedly correcting that response until it becomes reliable.

Fail Answer (exposes misunderstanding):

“Helping students understand their work better”
“Practicing what they learn in school”
“Improving performance through repetition”
Q2

A student understands fractions but freezes in tests.
Under TT:
👉 What exactly is the problem?

Expected Answer (signal):
The student’s response breaks under pressure in that arena. Understanding exists, but execution collapses when difficulty or pressure appears.

Fail Answer:

“They don’t understand properly”
“They lack confidence”
“They need more practice”
Q3

**Why is this statement wrong in TT:

“The student is bad at algebra”**

Expected Answer:
Because TT does not label ability globally. It identifies where the response breaks inside a specific arena. The student may be stable in parts and unstable in others.

Fail Answer:

“It’s too negative”
“It lowers confidence”
“It’s not motivating”
Q4

**What is the difference between:

“covering a topic”
“conditioning a topic”
Be precise.**

Expected Answer:
Covering = explaining and moving through content.
Conditioning = diagnosing where response breaks and training that response until it becomes reliable before progressing.

Fail Answer:

“Conditioning is just more practice”
“Covering is faster, conditioning is slower”
Q5

Why does TT refuse to move to a new topic just because the student “seems better”?

Expected Answer:
Because apparent improvement is not reliable execution. TT only moves once the student’s response is consistently stable under difficulty and pressure.

Fail Answer:

“Because we want to be thorough”
“Because repetition helps confidence”
🔹 SECTION B — APPLICATION
Q6

**Parent says:

“My child struggles with math in general”
What is your first move inside TT?**

Expected Answer:
Select a specific arena, test it using TT checks, and identify where the student’s response breaks.

Fail Answer:

“Ask what topics they don’t understand”
“Start teaching basics”

**You are given:

Topic: Linear Equations

Student delays starting
Student skips steps
👉 Identify:
Entry Phase
Why**

Expected Answer:
Structured Execution.
Because the student knows what to do but cannot act reliably and independently.

Fail Answer:

Clarity (wrong — clarity already present)
Controlled Discomfort (too advanced)
Q8

A student performs well in practice but collapses in exams.
👉 Which phase are you testing first and why?

Expected Answer:
Time Pressure Stability (and possibly Controlled Discomfort).
Because breakdown occurs under pressure, not in understanding or basic execution.

Fail Answer:

Clarity
“Go over the topic again”
Q9

**Student is:

High clarity
Good execution
Breaks when questions change form
👉 Where are they and what do you do next?**

Expected Answer:
Controlled Discomfort.
Introduce harder/unfamiliar problems and force stable response without rescue.

Fail Answer:

“Give more practice”
“Explain different question types”
Q10

**Why is it incorrect to say:

“This student is inconsistent”
Using TT logic.**

Expected Answer:
Because inconsistency is not random. It reflects different conditioning levels across arenas.

Fail Answer:

“It sounds negative”
“It hurts confidence”

performs well with guidance
collapses alone

Structured Execution.
Stop rescuing, guiding too much, or carrying them.

“They’re improving”
“Give more support”

student gets answers correct

Hold.
