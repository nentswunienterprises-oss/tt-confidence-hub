TT-OS BATTLE TEST: CONTROLLED DISCOMFORT (SCORING VERSION)
🔹 SECTION A -CORE UNDERSTANDING
Q1

What is the purpose of Controlled Discomfort in TT, in one sentence?

Expected Answer (signal):
To ensure the student can maintain structured execution when difficulty and uncertainty appear without relying on rescue.

Fail Answer:

“To challenge the student”
“To make them stronger”
“To build confidence”
Q2

Why does TT not allow full rescue in this phase?

Expected Answer:
Because rescue removes the very condition being trained-independent response under difficulty-and hides the breakdown.

Fail Answer:

“Because struggle helps learning”
“To push the student harder”
Q3

What is the difference between Structured Execution and Controlled Discomfort?

Expected Answer:
Structured Execution tests independent execution of a known method.
Controlled Discomfort tests whether that execution holds when uncertainty and difficulty are introduced.

Fail Answer:

“Discomfort is just harder questions”
“Execution is easier, discomfort is harder”
Q4

What does “hold the discomfort window” actually mean?

Expected Answer:
Allow the student to sit in difficulty without intervening, rescuing, or reducing pressure, so true response behavior can be observed.

Fail Answer:

“Give them time to think”
“Wait a bit before helping”
Q5

Why is only one-step confirmation allowed (and nothing more)?

Expected Answer:
To prevent full guidance while still anchoring the student enough to continue independently without collapsing.

Fail Answer:

“To help them get started”
“To make it easier”
🔹 SECTION B -APPLICATION
Q6

A student freezes when difficulty appears and immediately asks for help.
👉 What phase are they in and why?

Expected Answer:
Controlled Discomfort. The breakdown is happening under uncertainty, not clarity or execution.

Fail Answer:

Structured Execution
“They lack confidence”
Q7

During the discomfort window, the student says: “I don’t know.”
👉 What do you do next?

Expected Answer:
Hold the discomfort. Do not rescue. Require continued engagement or controlled first-step attempt.

Fail Answer:

“Explain the problem”
“Guide them through it”
Q8

A student completes the question correctly but required multiple hints during struggle.
👉 Do you accept or reject the rep? Why?

Expected Answer:
Reject. The response is not independent; it was supported.

Fail Answer:

“Accept, they got it right”
Q9

A student can execute well in familiar problems but collapses when the form changes.
👉 What is being exposed?

Expected Answer:
Failure under uncertainty-Controlled Discomfort breakdown.

Fail Answer:

“They don’t understand the topic”
“They need more practice”
Q10

Why does TT repeat the same level of difficulty multiple times in this phase?

Expected Answer:
To build stable tolerance to difficulty, not just survival of a single rep.

Fail Answer:

“To give more practice”
“To improve accuracy”
🔹 SECTION C -PRESSURE SCENARIOS
Scenario 1

Tutor sees the student struggling and says: “Let me show you how to do this.”
👉 What violation occurred?

Expected Answer:
Full rescue. The discomfort condition was removed.

Fail Answer:

“Helping the student understand”
Scenario 2

Student keeps asking for help after every step.
👉 What must you enforce?

Expected Answer:
No rescue. Maintain independence and require the student to continue without full guidance.

Fail Answer:

“Answer their questions to keep them moving”
Scenario 3

Tutor reduces the difficulty because the student looks overwhelmed.
👉 What is the problem?

Expected Answer:
The tutor is protecting comfort instead of training response under difficulty, breaking the phase.

Fail Answer:

“Adjusting to the student’s level”
Scenario 4

Student hesitates but eventually works through the problem independently without help.
👉 What does this indicate?

Expected Answer:
Improving discomfort tolerance and controlled response under difficulty.

Fail Answer:

“They’re gaining confidence”

❌ “Let’s reduce difficulty first”
→ Avoids the phase entirely
