TT-OS BATTLE TEST: CLARITY (SCORING VERSION)
🔹 SECTION A -CORE UNDERSTANDING
Q1

What is the purpose of Clarity in TT, in one sentence?

Expected Answer (signal):
To ensure the student can clearly recognize what they are dealing with (terms, type, steps, and reasoning) before solving begins.

Fail Answer:

“To help the student understand the topic”
“To explain the concept better”
“To build confidence”
Q2

Why is solving not allowed during Recognition sets?

Expected Answer:
Because the goal is to isolate recognition ability. Solving introduces execution noise and hides whether the student can actually see the problem clearly.

Fail Answer:

“To make it easier for the student”
“To simplify the process”
“Because we’re still teaching”
Q3

Explain the 3-Layer Lens and why all three layers must always be checked.

Expected Answer:
Vocabulary (naming), Method (steps), Reason (why). All must be checked because missing any layer means clarity is incomplete and response will break later.

Fail Answer:

Only mentions 1–2 layers
“It helps understanding” (too vague)
Q4

What does Clarity measure that normal tutoring ignores?

Expected Answer:
It measures whether the student can recognize, structure, and explain before solving—not just whether they can get answers.

Fail Answer:

“It checks understanding”
“It helps with basics”
Q5

Why does TT not allow skipping from Clarity to harder phases even if the student “seems smart”?

Expected Answer:
Because incomplete clarity leads to unstable execution later. The system requires full recognition before pressure is introduced.

Fail Answer:

“To be thorough”
“To make sure they don’t struggle”
🔹 SECTION B -APPLICATION
Q6

A student starts solving immediately but cannot name terms or explain steps.
👉 What phase are they in and why?

Expected Answer:
Clarity. They are executing without recognition, meaning they cannot see the problem properly.

Fail Answer:

Structured Execution
“They just need practice”
Q7

During a Recognition set, the student tries to solve anyway.
👉 What do you do immediately?

Expected Answer:
Stop the solving, reset the rep, and enforce recognition-only protocol.

Fail Answer:

“Let them continue”
“Help them finish”
Q8

A student can repeat steps but cannot explain why they work.
👉 What layer is weak and what does that mean?

Expected Answer:
Reason is weak. The student is memorizing without understanding, meaning clarity is incomplete.

Fail Answer:

“They understand enough”
“That’s fine for now”
Q9

In Light Apply, the student hesitates and delays starting.
👉 What is being exposed?

Expected Answer:
Clarity is not stable. The student cannot translate recognition into independent action.

Fail Answer:

“They lack confidence”
“They need motivation”
Q10

Why is minimal guidance enforced during Light Apply?

Expected Answer:
To test whether clarity holds under independent execution without tutor support.

Fail Answer:

“To make it harder”
“To challenge the student”
🔹 SECTION C -PRESSURE SCENARIOS
Scenario 1

Tutor explains Vocabulary → Method → Reason perfectly, but does not ask the student to explain back.
👉 What is wrong?

Expected Answer:
The student’s clarity is not verified. Without explanation back, recognition is assumed, not tested.

Fail Answer:

“Nothing, explanation is enough”
Scenario 2

Tutor turns Recognition reps into mini-teaching sessions.
👉 What violation is happening?

Expected Answer:
Breaking the drill protocol. Recognition sets must observe, not teach.

Fail Answer:

“Helping the student understand more”
Scenario 3

Student gets correct answers in Light Apply but uses vague language and unclear reasoning.
👉 Do you progress or hold? Why?

Expected Answer:
Hold. Correct answers do not equal clarity. Response is not reliable.

Fail Answer:

“Progress, they’re getting it right”
Scenario 4

Tutor skips Reason layer because Vocabulary and Method look strong.
👉 What is the risk?

Expected Answer:
Hidden instability. The student may execute but collapse when problems change or difficulty increases.

Fail Answer:

“It’s fine if they understand steps”
🔹 SECTION D -RED FLAG RESPONSES

→ Confuses outcome with recognition

❌ “I would just explain it better”
→ Defaults to teaching, not observing

❌ “We can move on once they get it”
→ Ignores stability requirement

❌ “Reason isn’t that important”
→ Breaks 3-Layer Lens

❌ “Let them solve, it shows understanding”
→ Breaks recognition protocol
